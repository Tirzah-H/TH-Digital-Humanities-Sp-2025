Alan Turing

The Father of Computer Theory

Alan Turing was a British computer scientist who created the Turing Machine, a predecessor to modern computers. It used the same logical principles of modern digital computers. He also coined the Turing Test, which states that if a computer can fool a human into thinking they are speaking to a human instead of a computer, then the computer is intelligent.

During WWII, Turing aided the allies in deciphering encoded transmissions from the Nazis. For this, he was made an Officer of the Most Excellent Order of the British Empire (OBE).

Homophobia

Turing was homosexual, which was illegal in the UK through the 1950s. After the war, he was charged with gross indecency for having a sexual relationship with a man. His security clearance was removed and he was forced to choose between prison and hormonal treatment. After two years, Turing died of cyanide poisoning, debatably a suicide.
